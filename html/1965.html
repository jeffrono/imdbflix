King Rat - (7.6 from 1619 votes)War | Drama

    IMDB Tagline: He entered WWII a soldier... and left a King.
    IMDB Comment: A gritty prison camp film, well directed and with great photography

Based on the novel by James Clavell, King Rat deals with a group of allied prisoners incarcerated in the infamous Japanese-run Changi POW camp in Singapore during World War II. In the camp, an American corporal (George Segal) with a genius for hustling does whatever he can -- including bribing guards -- to live as comfortably as possible, even at the expense of his fellow prisoners. Tom Courtenay, Patrick O'Neal and James Fox co-star.

    IMDB Tagline:
    IMDB Comment: Crime Story and Musical, Melodrama and Grotesque Comedy

Director Jean-Luc Godard's popular 1965 drama captures '60s French cool as only Godard could depict it. After attending a mindless party full of shallow chatter, Ferdinand (Jean-Paul Belmondo) suddenly feels a desperate need to escape and runs away with his baby-sitter, Marianna (Anna Karina). But Marianna's dark past still haunts her, and what follows is a roller-coaster ride filled with passion and desperation as the two lovers meet their fate.

Can she convince everyone that she's not insane?

Shenandoah - (7.3 from 2786 votes)Drama | War | Western

    IMDB Tagline: SHAKES THE SCREEN LIKE CANNON THUNDER! (original ad - all caps)
    IMDB Comment: Despite historical flaws this is a great movie!

A four-hankie weeper and one of the best melodramas to come out of Hollywood during the 1960s, Shenandoah concerns a pacifist Virginia farmer (Jimmy Stewart) who tries to keep his family out of harm's way during the Civil War. But when Stewart's youngest son is mistakenly imprisoned by Union soldiers, he must make some hard choices about where he stands in the bloody conflict.     IMDB Tagline: EXPLORER, MISTRESS, VAGRANT, LOAFER, ARTIST, TRAMP ... THEY ARE ALL AT THE CAPTAINS TABLE!
    IMDB Comment: Alltime favorite movie

Vivien Leigh, Simone Signoret, Jose Ferrer and Lee Marvin give riveting performances in this Stanley Kramer film adaptation of Katherine Anne Porter's 1962 novel. The film was Vivien Leigh's final screen appearance.
